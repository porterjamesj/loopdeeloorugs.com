Originally from Amityville, New York, I attended Syracuse University and graduated with a BFA in fabric design. Fresh out of college I landed a job in New York City working for a lively and very productive textile design studio. Surrounded by talented and interesting people, young and old, from all over the world, I really learned the ropes. Mixing colors, sketching and painting designs, reinterpreting old documents, traveling to southern mills to check production, this was work that I loved. Now located in rural northeast Ohio I continue to work as a textile designer from my home studio. Some of my designs appear in the following collections: Isaac Mizrahi for S. Harris, Jaclyn Smith Home, Charlotte Moss for

S. Harris, Mount Vernon Home for Fabricut, Vervain, Valdese Weavers, and Longaberger Company. Currently I am the creative director for the Thomas Strahan Collection of wallpapers.


My introduction to rug hooking was at our county fair. I was fascinated by a demonstration of this very old craft. The tactile quality of the rugs, the richness of color and variety in the hand-dyed wool are irresistible. After taking a beginner class I was most definitely “hooked.” Tea Garden was the first rug of my own design. I spent one whole summer cutting wool by hand. The edges were not straight on the grain (I didn’t know better) but it’s one of my favorite patterns still.


My hooked rug design is certainly influenced by the many years I’ve spend in the fabric industry. I love to explore the many different historic styles of surface pattern, revisiting these familiar classics with a fresh eye. I hope you enjoy them.
